KKday Rezio SaaS Service
Redesigning the OTA product listing experience

Product overview

KKday Rezio is a B2B booking and operations platform for travel businesses, covering product listing, payments, and order management. It integrates with channels such as GetYourGuide, Viator, KKday, and Activity Japan to expand distribution and streamline operations.

My Role:

As the lead interaction designer, I redesigned the end-to-end product listing experience by restructuring the information architecture, rebuilding the design system, and aligning teams around research findings. The redesign reduced setup time by 38% (45 → 28 min), achieved a SUS score of 80.3, and contributed to an estimated ~30% reduction in support ticket volume.

Project Background:

Originally built for a narrower user group, the product struggled as operational complexity grew. The "Getting Started" section alone drove 36% of all help article views, signaling deep setup friction. What was framed as a 10-minute setup routinely took 45-50 minutes, and 60% of users could not complete it without support.

Our Goal:

We redesigned the product listing experience to better match users' mental models by restructuring flows, rewriting labels, and surfacing guidance contextually, so users could complete setup with less confusion and less dependence on support.

03. Research and Problem Framing

Preliminary research:

We interviewed users and frontline staff to map how products were actually listed in practice. One charter business owner shared: "I miss the instant replies on LINE. With the new system, responses take much longer."

The pattern was consistent across interviews — system complexity had created a dependency on real-time support that neither users nor the support team could sustain.

Persona:

Research surfaced two distinct user types: Takeshi Tanaka, the business decision-maker optimizing for revenue and channel integration, and Mari Yamaguchi, the daily operator managing orders and reservations. Despite different goals, both hit the same wall — setup complexity that stalled progress and eroded trust in the system.

Revealing Gaps, Defining Priorities:

Card sorting exposed a fundamental mismatch between users' mental models and the product's information architecture. Usability testing quantified the impact: several core tasks logged completion rates below 50% and error rates above 2 per session — the strongest signal for where to direct the redesign.

Key Finding:

Usability testing identified 4 systemic friction sources in the legacy system:

Misaligned mental models — The listing flow was structured around system logic, not how users actually think about setting up their products.

No error recovery — Users hit dead ends with no in-context guidance, forcing them to abandon tasks or call support to continue.

Internal jargon — Labels reflected engineering concepts rather than user vocabulary, creating consistent misunderstanding at critical setup steps.

Inconsistent interaction patterns — Without a unified design language, users couldn't build reliable expectations as they moved through the interface.

Fail Fast, Succeed Faster:

In 3 months, we ran 5+ rounds of rolling research — advancing from concept sketches to a testable prototype. Each sprint was scoped to a specific hypothesis, ensuring every design change addressed a confirmed user finding rather than an assumption.

UI Mockups and Prototyping:

At each design review, we built prototypes — using AI generation or Figma — to test specific hypotheses before committing to development. The interactive prototype below covers the pricing flow: follow the green cursor to explore the interaction model.

04. Our Solution

New Proposal:

We decoupled pricing from identity-based time slot bindings — allowing users to assign price rules directly to time periods. The restructured flow follows users' natural working sequence, eliminating the abstract "identity relationship" concept that consistently blocked progress during testing.

Lowering Onboarding Cost with a Short Profiling Questionnaire

A short upfront questionnaire captures itinerary type, dynamically suppressing irrelevant fields throughout the flow. In usability testing, the questionnaire achieved 96% profiling accuracy — enabling users to complete setup end-to-end without backtracking or contacting support. The approach also reduced onboarding training time for the sales team.

Reducing Support Dependency with a Contextual Help Panel

The self-serve help panel surfaces contextual guidance at the exact point of friction — enabling users to resolve issues without contacting support. Estimated impact: ~30% reduction in support ticket volume and faster average resolution time, directly reducing operational cost.

UI Recording — Self-Serve Help Panel

Reducing Errors with Preview Before Publish

We replaced the single-column form with a dual-column interface: settings on the left, real-time product preview on the right. Every field change renders instantly — letting users validate their input before publishing and catching errors that previously required post-launch corrections.

Creating a More Conversational UX Copy System

We replaced isolated field labels with sentence-based inputs — embedding context directly into the form structure. Instead of interpreting a label in isolation, users read a complete sentence and fill in the blanks. Testing showed faster task completion and fewer misconfigurations, contributing directly to the overall SUS score of 80.3.

05. Results

The redesign delivered measurable progress against all three goals: setup time dropped from 45 to 28 minutes (–38%), SUS reached 80.3, and support ticket volume was estimated to decrease by ~30% without additional headcount.

AI-assisted prototyping shortened the iteration cycle, while the shared design system reduced recurring handoff friction between design and engineering — improvements that continued beyond the project itself.

With more time, I would involve users earlier in stakeholder alignment so evidence could shape consensus sooner and reduce rework. Even so, one of the strongest outcomes was the team capability built through the process itself.
